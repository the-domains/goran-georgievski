1. Nuke or Dr Strangelove lost in the Giant Mountain

Hidden in the sleepy underground below the mountain pass on the border between Poland and Czech Republic, this mine is a real gem for the adventure loving souls. Discovered after Second World War it was exploited during 50-60s years of the past century to support Soviet Union nuclear program. In the 70s Uranium was exhausted, and the mine was turned into Radon spa - inhalatorium. Radon vapor deceased in 1989. Since then the mine was closed and forgotten up to 2014, when is open for public. More pictures from this fascinating, apocalyptic and surreal place telling stories about the Cold War from behind the Iron Curtain can be seen here http://www.kopalniapodgorze.pl/index.html  as well as some info about opening hours and entrance prices.
